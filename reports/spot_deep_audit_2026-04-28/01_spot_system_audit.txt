Spot System Audit

1. Executive read

The live spot lane is currently a hybrid system:
- it inherits candidate ideas from the broad crypto scanner and lane-routing layer
- it adds a mathematically richer spot state on top
- it then executes on Coinbase spot with maker-first / taker-fallback logic

That sounds strong in theory, but the actual live implementation has drifted into a weak shape:
- most live spot entries are low-quality opportunistic pullback trades
- setup scores are low
- the main quality gates are mostly disabled
- the system is relying on exits to rescue weak entries
- the entries usually do not develop enough follow-through to clear fees

2. Current live spot philosophy as implemented

2.1 Candidate feed philosophy

The live spot lane is not sourced from a fully native Coinbase-spot scanner.
It is fed by:
- the broad crypto scanner
- tradeability routing
- spot-only synthetic candidate injection for names the perp scanner does not naturally cover

Evidence
- scheduler/v10_runner.py injects synthetic LONG candidates for spot-only names
- recent entered spot candidates include many PF_ symbols from Kraken/perp-style feeds

  - SOL: 33 total, 22 PF_-style
  - ETH: 20 total, 18 PF_-style

  - BTC/DOGE/ADA/LINK/LTC: mostly direct base symbols

Interpretation
- a meaningful share of live spot entries are originating from perp-style symbol feeds and scanner context, then being translated into Coinbase spot execution
- this is not automatically wrong, but it means spot execution is inheriting non-spot microstructure context

2.2 Signal philosophy

The spot lane uses two score families:

A. Existing composite score
- comes from signal_engine.py
- built from technical score + ML score

B. Spot derivative score
- built in runtime/spot_momentum.py
- uses:
  - z state
  - velocity v
  - acceleration a
  - jerk j
  - momentum integral
  - acceleration integral
  - path efficiency
  - structure
  - participation
  - volatility quality

This is real math, not fake window dressing.
The spot derivative layer is genuinely more advanced than the old simple momentum stack.

2.3 Spot state math

The spot derivative engine builds per-timeframe state from:
- KST spread
- MACD histogram
- AVWAP/VWAP deviation
- Ichimoku cloud state
- SuperTrend state
- log returns
- WAE
- squeeze state
- dollar volume
- ADX
- rv_ratio
- OU half-life
- autocorrelation

Frame score formula
- base anchor: 55
- then weighted contributions from z, v, a, j, impulse integrals, structure, participation, volatility quality, and path efficiency

Important conclusion
- the math stack is sophisticated enough to support a good spot engine
- the current problem is not "insufficient calculus"
- the current problem is how that math is being gated, blended, and operationalized

8. Math diagnosis

What is good:
- the spot state math is real
- multi-timeframe derivative state is real
- structure, participation, and volatility features are present

What is not good:
- the advanced math is under-constrained by the live policy
- the spot lane does not require strong setup scores
- higher final_spot_score buckets have not translated into better recent outcomes
- most recent trades happened in NEUTRAL regime, not TREND

Recent entered spot regimes:
- NEUTRAL: 117
- CHOP: 24
- TREND: 2

Interpretation
- the system claims to be a burst-finding scalp engine
- but recent live behavior has mostly been NEUTRAL pullback trading
- that is a philosophical mismatch

9. Bottom-line system diagnosis

The current spot system is not failing because it lacks sophistication.
It is failing because:
- the live decision policy is admitting too many low-quality opportunistic reclaim setups
- the score stack is not calibrated to immediate follow-through
- the route and fee burden are strong enough to turn nearly-flat gross edge into guaranteed negative net edge
- the real learning loops are not actually closing the feedback loop on recent spot trades
