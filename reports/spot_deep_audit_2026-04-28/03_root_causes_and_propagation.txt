Root Causes And Propagation

This file enumerates the major root causes behind current spot underperformance and shows how each one propagates through the codebase and operational workflows.

RC1. Spot closes do not enter the learning loop

Root cause
- spot_engine.close_spot() logs and deletes positions, but does not call:
  - learning_loop.record_closed_trade()
  - learning.post_trade_analyzer.analyze_closed_trade()

Evidence
- recent spot attribution rows after 2026-04-22T21:36:39Z: 0
- all spot attribution rows: 0
- recent spot ML snapshots after cutoff: 0

Propagation
- no recent spot feature snapshots
- no recent spot trade attribution
- no spot Bayesian updates
- no spot retrain queue growth from recent closes
- no spot RBI incubation feedback
- dashboard learning surfaces overstate health by looking at older/global data

Why it matters
- the system cannot adapt from the exact live failure streak that should be teaching it the most

RC2. Spot edge calibration is structurally incapable of reconstructing many real spot trades

Root cause
- calibrator joins scan_candidates to SELL trade timestamps, not entry timestamps
- join also requires exact symbol equality

Evidence
- calibrator-style match rate over real live spot sells: 48.9%
- ETH: 1 match out of 19 sells
- LTC: 0 matches out of 4 sells
- spot_edge_conditions rows: 0

Propagation
- derived conditions never populate
- runtime/spot_strategy.py falls back to config/open-gate policy
- the supposedly adaptive spot-edge layer stays inert

Why it matters
- the bot keeps trading generic permissive config rather than empirically derived edge rules

RC3. Candidate priors are learning the wrong target for the live spot lane

Root cause
- entry priors define a win as:
  - hit_1r = 1
  - and hit_stop = 0
- the live spot lane behaves more like:
  - get in quickly
  - capture fast follow-through
  - exit on thesis decay if momentum stalls

Evidence
- recent entered spot candidate outcomes:
  - hit_0.5R at a decent rate in some names
  - almost never hit 1R
  - frequent stop / decay behavior
- recent closes overwhelmingly end by thesis_decay

Propagation
- economics gate uses win-rate priors from a target that is not aligned with the live scalp objective
- priors can look mathematically solid while still being strategically off-target

Why it matters
- the lane can admit trades that look acceptable under a 1R-based prior, but are bad for a fast fee-sensitive scalp

RC4. Setup language mismatch between priors and the spot engine

Root cause
- entry priors bucket by scanner primary_setup
- live spot lane gates by spot setup_family and setup_score

Evidence
- recent entered mappings:
  - ranging_mr -> pullback_reclaim
  - momentum -> pullback_reclaim
  - kst_cross -> pullback_reclaim

Propagation
- learning buckets and execution buckets do not describe the same trade idea
- even good prior data may not refine the actual spot gate that fired

Why it matters
- it weakens every attempt at feedback-driven refinement

RC5. Live ML inference is stale and generic

Root cause
- model files are stale
- signal_engine does not pass symbol into ModelStore inference

Evidence
- latest model artifacts dated 2026-04-13
- _get_ml_score() calls predict_ml_score(features, direction) without symbol

Propagation
- ML score is not truly symbol-aware at inference
- recent spot damage does not reshape live ML behavior

Why it matters
- the lane looks ML-assisted, but that assistance is stale and generic

RC6. Online learner exists but is unused

Root cause
- ml/online_learner.py is not called anywhere

Propagation
- there is no between-retrain adaptation
- the system cannot quickly learn "stop doing this exact thing" after a losing streak

Why it matters
- this is one reason the bot kept repeating similar weak trades

RC7. The gate philosophy is too permissive for the actual fee burden

Root cause
- most quality gates are off
- setup_score is not a hard edge gate
- recent live floors were lowered materially

Evidence
- entered spot setup_family:
  - 142/143 = pullback_reclaim
- entered setup_preference:
  - 142/143 = opportunistic
- avg setup_score:
  - 0.2766 for pullback_reclaim
- current open positions still have setup_score as low as 0.1516

Propagation
- advanced spot math becomes advisory rather than decisive
- the entry layer admits a large number of mediocre opportunities
- exits are forced into cleanup mode

Why it matters
- this is the most direct reason volume rose while edge fell

RC8. The system is mostly trading NEUTRAL pullback setups, not strong burst setups

Root cause
- live entries are dominated by pullback_reclaim in NEUTRAL

Evidence
- recent spot regimes:
  - NEUTRAL 117
  - CHOP 24
  - TREND 2

Propagation
- the lane is not primarily harvesting obvious burst continuation
- it is mostly trying to reclaim into weak/neutral context

Why it matters
- this conflicts with the intended "get in, go up, get out" philosophy

RC9. The gross edge is too small to survive Coinbase spot fees

Root cause
- recent trades barely make money even before fees

Evidence
- avg gross pnl before fees: -0.0490
- avg net pnl after fees: -1.7760
- avg round-trip fee: 1.1349
- gross positive rate: 42.45%
- net positive rate: 0.00%

Propagation
- even when direction is somewhat right, the fee load dominates
- route improvements alone cannot rescue the lane unless the pre-fee move quality improves too

Why it matters
- the system currently does not have enough pre-fee edge

RC10. The economics gate checks target positivity, not empirical expected value

Root cause
- spot economics gate approves when:
  - theoretical net target is positive
  - projected net win is bigger than fees
- it does not use empirical fast-follow-through probability directly

Evidence
- recent entered candidates were often approved with:
  - positive projected net win
  - but realized average gross pnl still near zero or negative

Propagation
- trades can pass economics as "possible winners" even when their actual short-horizon expectancy is poor

Why it matters
- the gate is mathematically coherent but strategically incomplete

RC11. Monitoring hides some of the failure state

Root cause
- nightly audit passes some broken checks
- attribution coverage and learning health are using mismatched scopes

Evidence
- ml_retrain_queue check uses a nonexistent status column
- latest audits report learning_health pass despite zero recent spot learning
- attribution coverage shows absurd percentages

Propagation
- operator trust is damaged
- critical learning failures are easier to miss

Why it matters
- systems engineering failure can prolong trading failure

RC12. Synthetic / PF_-translated spot lineage adds avoidable complexity

Root cause
- spot entries often originate under PF_ symbols and scanner exchange contexts

Evidence
- SOL: 22/33 entered spot candidates were PF_
- ETH: 18/20 entered spot candidates were PF_

Propagation
- symbol normalization becomes fragile
- calibrator lineage becomes harder
- priors and outcomes are recorded in a scanner language different from executed spot holdings

Why it matters
- this is not the biggest loss driver by itself, but it amplifies learning and lineage fragility

Bottom-line cause tree

Primary causes
- broken spot learning ingestion
- non-operational spot calibration
- wrong target for candidate priors
- permissive entry philosophy
- insufficient pre-fee edge

Secondary causes
- stale generic ML inference
- unused online learner
- monitoring blind spots
- symbol and setup-language mismatches

Net effect
- more trades
- mostly mediocre opportunistic pullback entries
- little true recent adaptation
- fees converting weak gross edge into universal net losses
