Training And Edge Repair Plan

1. What the system actually needs

The live spot lane does not primarily need more indicator complexity.
It needs:
- truthful ingestion of recent spot outcomes
- correct lineage between entries and outcomes
- targets aligned with fast fee-cleared follow-through
- monitoring that stops lying by omission

3. What to train on

The current priors are learning a clean 1R winner label.
That is not the best target for this spot lane.

Replace or add the following learning targets:

Target A. fast_fee_cleared_follow_through
- 1 if the trade achieves enough favorable excursion quickly enough to cover:
  - both fees
  - spread
  - expected slippage
- 0 otherwise

Target B. thesis_decay_risk
- probability the trade loses derivative support before producing a useful move

Target C. expected_net_pnl_after_fees
- a regression target, not just classification

Target D. route_conditional_value
- separate expectation for:
  - maker_first
  - taker_fallback

4. Modeling philosophy

4.1 Use two learning layers, not one

Layer 1. Candidate prior layer
- purpose:
  early probability estimate before execution
- inputs:
  candidate labeler outcomes
  scanner context
  coarse regime and setup context

Layer 2. Trade truth layer
- purpose:
  what actually survives live execution and fees
- inputs:
  real closed-trade outcomes
  actual route
  actual fees
  actual stop/target geometry
  actual derivative state

The recent spot failure happened partly because Layer 1 remained alive while Layer 2 was broken.

4.2 Use symbol-shrunk models, not fully pooled or fully isolated

Recommended approach
- shared base model across spot assets
- symbol-specific offsets or hierarchical shrinkage
- route-specific adjustments

Why
- full per-symbol isolation overfits
- full pooling ignores real symbol differences

5. What would actually improve win rate

Blunt truth
- with the current feature-to-entry mapping, win rate improves mainly by trading less
- but the goal should be better net expectancy first, not raw WR vanity

If win rate must rise materially, the highest-probability levers are:
- stop admitting low-setup-score opportunistic pullback trades
- reduce NEUTRAL reclaim volume
- tighten taker admission
- require stronger fast-follow-through evidence for pullback_reclaim specifically

Recent evidence supporting that:
- 142 of 143 entered spot candidates were pullback_reclaim
- avg setup_score was only 0.2766
- 117 of 143 entries happened in NEUTRAL
- 123 closes ended in thesis_decay

So the path to higher WR is not "raise every score floor blindly."
It is:
- stop treating weak reclaims as acceptable spot scalps
- retrain around fast follow-through and thesis-decay avoidance

6. What would improve profit edge even if win rate stayed moderate

These are the best edge levers:
- maker-dominant execution on marginal setups
- stronger fee-aware target selection by route
- suppress symbols with poor gross edge
- suppress setups whose average 15m/1h follow-through is weak
- optimize for:
  expected net dollars, not just score or nominal 1R hit rate

7. Temporary operational posture while retraining

Until the learning and lineage fixes are complete:
- treat live spot as partially blind
- prefer low-churn, higher-conviction entries
- heavily penalize taker fallback
- consider temporarily suppressing the worst recent symbol/setup pockets

8. Most important final conclusion

The best next edge does not come from inventing more math.
It comes from making the existing math observable, teachable, and aligned to the actual scalp objective.

The system already has enough feature richness.
What it lacks is:
- truthful ingestion
- aligned targets
- proper lineage
- and enough respect for the difference between gross edge and net edge.
